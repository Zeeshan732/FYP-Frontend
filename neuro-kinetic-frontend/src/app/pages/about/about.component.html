Our mission

Early detection saves lives

Parkinson's disease affects over 10 million people worldwide. The earlier it is detected, the more effective treatment becomes. NeuroSync was built to close the gap between symptom onset and diagnosis — using signals the human body already produces every day.

Our AI models analyse subtle changes in voice tremor, walking patterns, and finger-tapping rhythm that often appear years before a clinical diagnosis is possible.

"

The average time between first symptoms and a Parkinson's diagnosis is over 2 years. We believe AI can change that — not by replacing neurologists, but by giving them earlier, richer data to work with.

How it works

Three signals. One assessment.

01

Voice analysis

Record a 10-second voice sample. The AI detects pitch variance, tremor frequency, and speech irregularities linked to motor dysfunction.

Voice signal
02

Gait analysis

Upload a short walking video or sensor CSV. The model measures stride length, cadence, and asymmetry — key Parkinson's indicators.

Gait signal
03

Finger tapping

An interactive 10-second tap test captures rhythm variance and bradykinesia — slowed repetitive movement is one of the earliest Parkinson's signs.
